NEVER JUST TURN YOUR SYSTEM OFF

Always shut your system down from the Desktop.

By shutting down from the Desktop you will ensure that:

    o

      Any new or altered files that were being compressed in the background will be compressed and saved before the system is stopped.
    o
      ZipStream is shutdown correctly which in turn will ensure that any changed configuration information is preserved.
    o
      ZipStream is idle at shutdown, ensuring the most reliable operation of your system.

    During the shutdown process, ZipStream will display an Information Window informing you that it is shutting down.

    If there are remaining compression jobs outstanding, the window will show a count of the jobs and will update this counter until all jobs have been completed.

    Using Ctrl-Alt-Del

    We recommend strongly that this method of shutdown is used only when OS/2 fails to shutdown in the normal manner, or for some reason the Desktop is no longer accessible.

    ZipStream will sense this event and close all open files and abort any operations that may have been in progress, before allowing the system to stop.

    This ensures the safe and reliable operation of your system.

    Note: Using Ctrl-Alt-Del may cause files that were queued to be compressed to remain uncompressed, however no damage to the files should occur.
