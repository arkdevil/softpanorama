If you are capable of running OS/2 2.1 or higher, you can take advantage of ZipStream.

In case you're still unsure, ZipStream has the following requirements:

    o

      A PC that is supported, and is capable of running OS/2 2.1 or higher.
    o
      At least 4 MB of RAM, 8 MB recommended, 16 MB perfect.
    o
      At least 10 MB of swap area is recommended (see below for more info on Swap Requirements).
    o
      A 386 or better CPU, 486 highly recommended, Pentium outstanding.

    Recommendations

    ZipStream excels on 486's with 16 MB of RAM, and for that matter, so does OS/2 2.1.

    ZipStream has been designed to take advantage of 486 (and better) CPU's, and ZipStreams large sparse virtual memory management system performs best when there is plenty of RAM.

    The benefit of these recommendations is that ZipStream gives access performance to compressed files as if they were not compressed. For example, after a small initial access delay to game files, games will play with no noticeable degradation in performance.

    Swap File Requirements

    ZipStream is designed to give fast access to files by trying to keep as many of the current set of files decompressed in memory as possible (Note: ZipStream's behaviour is highly configurable in this area).

    On systems with low amounts of RAM, ZipStream will utilise Swap Space to keep uncompressed information available for ready access. More memory means less Swap requirements and faster access. More files being concurrently accessed, means more Memory requirements.

    Of course, the Swap Space requirements are easily reclaimed MANY times over by keeping your files compressed with ZipStream.
